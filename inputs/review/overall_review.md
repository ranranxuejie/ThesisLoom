## I. Universal Writing Taboos Across Entire Manuscript

### 1. Logical Prohibitions

- **DO NOT** introduce research questions in later sections that were not stated in the Introduction
- **DO NOT** present findings in Results that do not correspond to Methods described
- **DO NOT** make claims in Discussion that cannot be traced back to presented Results
- **DO NOT** state conclusions in Conclusion that contradict findings in Results
- **DO NOT** change terminology for the same concept across different sections
- **DO NOT** introduce new hypotheses in Discussion that were not tested in the study
- **DO NOT** present causal claims from observational or correlational study designs
- **DO NOT** ignore or contradict your own stated limitations when making conclusions
- **DO NOT** switch research focus midway through the manuscript without justification
- **DO NOT** present selective findings that contradict the complete analytical plan

### 2. Structural Prohibitions

- **DO NOT** exceed journal-specified word counts for any section
- **DO NOT** omit required sections (Abstract, Methods, Results, Discussion, References)
- **DO NOT** place Results content within Discussion section
- **DO NOT** place interpretive content within Results section
- **DO NOT** include figures or tables that are not referenced in the text
- **DO NOT** reference figures or tables that do not exist
- **DO NOT** use inconsistent heading hierarchy or formatting throughout
- **DO NOT** submit manuscripts without required ethical approval statements
- **DO NOT** omit conflict of interest declarations when conflicts exist
- **DO NOT** fail to include data availability statements when required

### 3. Language and Expression Prohibitions

- **DO NOT** use first-person informal language ("I think," "I believe," "In my opinion")
- **DO NOT** use colloquial expressions, slang, or conversational tone
- **DO NOT** use emotional or promotional language ("amazing," "revolutionary," "perfect," "unprecedented")
- **DO NOT** use absolute terms without qualification ("always," "never," "proves," "definitely")
- **DO NOT** use vague quantifiers without specific numbers ("many," "some," "several," "a lot")
- **DO NOT** use undefined abbreviations or acronyms
- **DO NOT** mix tense inconsistently within the same section
- **DO NOT** use passive voice exclusively when active voice improves clarity
- **DO NOT** write overly long sentences with multiple nested clauses
- **DO NOT** use rhetorical questions in formal academic writing

### 4. Attitude and Tone Prohibitions

- **DO NOT** disparage or dismiss prior work without evidence
- **DO NOT** overstate novelty claims without thorough literature verification
- **DO NOT** make personal attacks on other researchers or their work
- **DO NOT** express frustration or disappointment about findings
- **DO NOT** use defensive language when discussing limitations
- **DO NOT** make promotional or marketing-style claims about your work
- **DO NOT** express certainty beyond what the data supports
- **DO NOT** ignore contradictory evidence or conflicting literature
- **DO NOT** write with bias toward confirming your hypothesis
- **DO NOT** minimize or hide unfavorable findings

## II. Section-Specific Common Errors and Prohibitions

### 1. Abstract Prohibitions

- **DO NOT** include information not present in the main manuscript
- **DO NOT** cite references within the abstract
- **DO NOT** use abbreviations without defining them first
- **DO NOT** present detailed statistical methods
- **DO NOT** make claims stronger than what the full paper supports
- **DO NOT** omit key numerical results with effect sizes
- **DO NOT** exceed abstract word limits specified by journal
- **DO NOT** write abstract after completing other sections without verification of consistency
- **DO NOT** include background information that is not directly relevant to the study
- **DO NOT** end abstract without clear conclusion or implication statement

### 2. Introduction Prohibitions

- **DO NOT** begin with overly broad or generic statements unrelated to the specific field
- **DO NOT** cite outdated literature when recent work exists
- **DO NOT** fail to clearly articulate the research gap
- **DO NOT** state research objectives that are vague or unmeasurable
- **DO NOT** preview findings or conclusions in the Introduction
- **DO NOT** write Introduction longer than 15-20% of total manuscript
- **DO NOT** include methodological details belonging in Methods section
- **DO NOT** make contribution claims that cannot be delivered in the paper
- **DO NOT** fail to connect research gap to stated objectives
- **DO NOT** omit statement of study hypothesis or research question

### 3. Methods Prohibitions

- **DO NOT** omit critical details needed for independent replication
- **DO NOT** describe procedures that were not actually performed
- **DO NOT** fail to report ethical approval and consent procedures
- **DO NOT** omit sample size justification or power calculations
- **DO NOT** use proprietary methods without sufficient description
- **DO NOT** fail to specify software versions and statistical packages
- **DO NOT** describe analytical methods not used in Results
- **DO NOT** omit manufacturer details for equipment and reagents
- **DO NOT** fail to report modifications to established protocols
- **DO NOT** include Results or interpretive content in Methods section

### 4. Results Prohibitions

- **DO NOT** include interpretation, speculation, or explanation of findings
- **DO NOT** compare results with prior literature
- **DO NOT** omit non-significant or negative findings that were pre-specified
- **DO NOT** report p-values only as thresholds (p<0.05) without exact values
- **DO NOT** present data not collected or analyzed according to Methods
- **DO NOT** include figures or tables with incomplete or missing labels
- **DO NOT** fail to report sample sizes (n) for each analysis
- **DO NOT** present confidence intervals without specifying the level (e.g., 95%)
- **DO NOT** round numbers inconsistently throughout the section
- **DO NOT** discuss implications or recommendations in Results section

### 5. Conclusion Prohibitions

- **DO NOT** introduce new data or findings not presented in Results
- **DO NOT** make recommendations beyond what the evidence supports
- **DO NOT** repeat Discussion content verbatim
- **DO NOT** fail to connect back to Introduction research question
- **DO NOT** overstate implications beyond study scope
- **DO NOT** omit acknowledgment of key limitations
- **DO NOT** write Conclusion longer than 10% of total manuscript
- **DO NOT** end with vague or generic statements
- **DO NOT** make clinical or policy recommendations without appropriate evidence level
- **DO NOT** fail to provide clear final takeaway message

## III. Discussion Section: All Red Lines and Absolute Prohibitions

### 1. Prohibition on Repeating Results

- **DO NOT** restate specific numbers, percentages, or statistics from Results
- **DO NOT** repeat p-values, confidence intervals, or effect sizes already reported
- **DO NOT** describe figures or tables in detail (already done in Results)
- **DO NOT** present new data or findings not shown in Results section
- **DO NOT** quote Results section text verbatim in Discussion
- **DO NOT** use Results section language patterns in Discussion opening
- **DO NOT** spend more than one paragraph summarizing findings
- **DO NOT** include statistical test descriptions belonging in Methods

### 2. Prohibition on Over-interpretation and Exaggerated Conclusions

- **DO NOT** claim findings "prove" anything (use "suggest," "indicate," "demonstrate")
- **DO NOT** state conclusions with more certainty than the data supports
- **DO NOT** use absolute language ("always," "never," "definitely," "certainly")
- **DO NOT** claim "first ever" without thorough literature verification
- **DO NOT** use promotional terms ("revolutionary," "groundbreaking," "unprecedented")
- **DO NOT** overstate clinical or practical applicability without validation
- **DO NOT** claim findings apply to populations not studied
- **DO NOT** generalize beyond the scope of your study design
- **DO NOT** make claims that cannot be traced to specific Results
- **DO NOT** ignore effect size magnitude when discussing significance

### 3. Prohibition on Unsupported Causal Inference

- **DO NOT** claim causality from observational or correlational study designs
- **DO NOT** use "causes," "caused by," "leads to" without RCT evidence
- **DO NOT** imply causal mechanisms without experimental validation
- **DO NOT** state that one variable "determines" another without appropriate design
- **DO NOT** use causal language when only association was tested
- **DO NOT** ignore alternative explanations for observed associations
- **DO NOT** claim intervention effects from non-interventional studies
- **DO NOT** suggest treatment recommendations from exploratory analyses
- **DO NOT** fail to acknowledge when causality cannot be inferred
- **DO NOT** use causal diagrams or language without causal study design

### 4. Prohibition on Avoiding Limitations

- **DO NOT** omit a dedicated limitations paragraph or section
- **DO NOT** bury limitations in one sentence at the end of Discussion
- **DO NOT** minimize or downplay major methodological weaknesses
- **DO NOT** fail to acknowledge how limitations affect interpretation
- **DO NOT** hide limitations that could affect study validity
- **DO NOT** claim limitations do not matter without justification
- **DO NOT** fail to connect limitations to specific future research directions
- **DO NOT** acknowledge limitations only to immediately dismiss them
- **DO NOT** omit discussion of potential biases in study design
- **DO NOT** fail to address generalizability constraints

### 5. Prohibition on Subjective and Emotional Expression

- **DO NOT** use first-person opinion statements ("I believe," "I feel," "In my view")
- **DO NOT** express surprise, disappointment, or excitement about findings
- **DO NOT** use emotional adjectives ("surprisingly," "unfortunately," "interestingly")
- **DO NOT** make value judgments about findings ("good," "bad," "better," "worse")
- **DO NOT** express personal preferences or biases
- **DO NOT** use defensive language when discussing contradictory findings
- **DO NOT** make personal comments about other researchers
- **DO NOT** express frustration with prior work or the field
- **DO NOT** use exclamation points or emphatic punctuation
- **DO NOT** write in conversational or informal tone

### 6. Prohibition on Logical Contradictions with Introduction/Results

- **DO NOT** address research questions not stated in Introduction
- **DO NOT** contradict findings presented in Results section
- **DO NOT** change the study's stated objectives mid-manuscript
- **DO NOT** ignore hypotheses stated in Introduction when discussing findings
- **DO NOT** claim contributions that do not match Introduction promises
- **DO NOT** discuss variables not measured or analyzed in the study
- **DO NOT** fail to answer the research question posed in Introduction
- **DO NOT** introduce new theoretical frameworks not mentioned earlier
- **DO NOT** contradict methodological limitations stated in Methods
- **DO NOT** make claims inconsistent with your own reported data

### 7. Prohibition on Vague and Empty Statements

- **DO NOT** end with generic statements like "more research is needed"
- **DO NOT** provide non-specific future directions without concrete suggestions
- **DO NOT** write vague implications without specifying beneficiaries
- **DO NOT** use empty phrases ("this is important," "this matters," "this is significant")
- **DO NOT** fail to specify what type of future research is needed
- **DO NOT** write conclusions that could apply to any study in the field
- **DO NOT** omit specific populations, settings, or conditions for applicability
- **DO NOT** make broad claims without specifying boundaries
- **DO NOT** use filler content to meet word count requirements
- **DO NOT** write Discussion paragraphs without clear main points

## IV. Academic Misconduct, Imprecision, and Non-Compliance

### 1. Academic Integrity Violations

- **DO NOT** fabricate, falsify, or manipulate data or results
- **DO NOT** plagiarize text, ideas, or figures from other sources
- **DO NOT** submit the same manuscript to multiple journals simultaneously
- **DO NOT** publish the same findings in multiple papers without cross-reference
- **DO NOT** omit authors who made substantial contributions
- **DO NOT** include authors who did not make substantial contributions
- **DO NOT** fail to disclose conflicts of interest
- **DO NOT** use AI-generated text without disclosure when required
- **DO NOT** copy methods descriptions from other papers without attribution
- **DO NOT** present others' unpublished work without permission

### 2. Statistical and Data Reporting Violations

- **DO NOT** engage in p-hacking (running multiple tests until significance is found)
- **DO NOT** engage in HARKing (hypothesizing after results are known)
- **DO NOT** selectively report only significant findings
- **DO NOT** omit details about data exclusions or missing data
- **DO NOT** report incorrect statistical tests for the data type
- **DO NOT** fail to correct for multiple comparisons when required
- **DO NOT** misrepresent confidence intervals or error bars
- **DO NOT** use inappropriate measures of central tendency
- **DO NOT** fail to report effect sizes alongside p-values
- **DO NOT** manipulate figures or images to misrepresent data

### 3. Ethical and Regulatory Violations

- **DO NOT** conduct research without required ethical approval
- **DO NOT** fail to obtain informed consent from participants
- **DO NOT** violate patient privacy or confidentiality
- **DO NOT** use animal subjects without proper ethical oversight
- **DO NOT** fail to register clinical trials before enrollment
- **DO NOT** omit funding source declarations
- **DO NOT** fail to disclose industry relationships
- **DO NOT** use protected data without proper authorization
- **DO NOT** violate data sharing agreements or restrictions
- **DO NOT** fail to report adverse events in clinical studies

### 4. Citation and Reference Violations

- **DO NOT** cite sources you have not actually read
- **DO NOT** cite references that do not support your claims
- **DO NOT** fail to cite seminal or foundational work in the field
- **DO NOT** excessively cite your own previous work without relevance
- **DO NOT** omit citations for ideas or methods from other researchers
- **DO NOT** use outdated references when current work exists
- **DO NOT** cite predatory or low-quality journals without justification
- **DO NOT** fail to verify citation accuracy before submission
- **DO NOT** use citation manipulation to boost metrics
- **DO NOT** copy reference lists from other papers

### 5. Formatting and Compliance Violations

- **DO NOT** ignore journal-specific formatting requirements
- **DO NOT** fail to follow reporting guidelines (CONSORT, STROBE, PRISMA, etc.)
- **DO NOT** submit figures at incorrect resolution or format
- **DO NOT** exceed file size limits for submissions
- **DO NOT** fail to include required supplementary materials
- **DO NOT** ignore word count restrictions
- **DO NOT** use incorrect reference style for target journal
- **DO NOT** fail to include required declarations (ethics, conflicts, data)
- **DO NOT** submit without all co-author approvals
- **DO NOT** ignore peer review feedback without response

## V. Top-Tier Journal Reviewer Most-Disliked Issues

### 1. Reviewer Red Flag #1: Overclaiming

- **DO NOT** claim novelty without thorough literature comparison
- **DO NOT** overstate implications beyond what data supports
- **DO NOT** make grandiose claims about field-changing impact
- **DO NOT** claim clinical applicability without appropriate evidence
- **DO NOT** suggest your work solves major unsolved problems without validation

### 2. Reviewer Red Flag #2: Methodological Opacity

- **DO NOT** provide insufficient methods detail for replication
- **DO NOT** fail to justify sample size or statistical power
- **DO NOT** omit critical control experiments or validations
- **DO NOT** use inappropriate statistical methods without justification
- **DO NOT** fail to address potential confounding variables

### 3. Reviewer Red Flag #3: Literature Engagement Failure

- **DO NOT** ignore key prior work in the field
- **DO NOT** fail to explain how your work differs from prior studies
- **DO NOT** dismiss contradictory findings without engagement
- **DO NOT** cite only old literature without recent references
- **DO NOT** fail to position your work within current field debates

### 4. Reviewer Red Flag #4: Results-Discussion Boundary Violation

- **DO NOT** include interpretation in Results section
- **DO NOT** present new data in Discussion section
- **DO NOT** repeat Results statistics in Discussion
- **DO NOT** fail to clearly separate findings from interpretation
- **DO NOT** mix Methods content into Results or Discussion

### 5. Reviewer Red Flag #5: Limitation Avoidance

- **DO NOT** fail to acknowledge major study weaknesses
- **DO NOT** dismiss limitations without thoughtful consideration
- **DO NOT** hide limitations that affect validity or generalizability
- **DO NOT** fail to discuss how limitations affect conclusions
- **DO NOT** pretend limitations do not exist

### 6. Reviewer Red Flag #6: Writing Quality Issues

- **DO NOT** submit with obvious grammatical or spelling errors
- **DO NOT** write unclear or ambiguous sentences
- **DO NOT** use inconsistent terminology throughout manuscript
- **DO NOT** fail to proofread before submission
- **DO NOT** submit with poor figure or table quality

### 7. Reviewer Red Flag #7: Logical Incoherence

- **DO NOT** present findings that do not address stated research questions
- **DO NOT** draw conclusions not supported by presented Results
- **DO NOT** fail to connect Introduction gap to Discussion conclusions
- **DO NOT** write sections that could be rearranged without affecting logic
- **DO NOT** submit manuscript without clear narrative arc

### 8. Reviewer Red Flag #8: Ethical Concerns

- **DO NOT** fail to disclose conflicts of interest
- **DO NOT** omit ethical approval statements
- **DO NOT** present data that raises privacy concerns
- **DO NOT** fail to acknowledge contributions appropriately
- **DO NOT** submit work with questionable data integrity
